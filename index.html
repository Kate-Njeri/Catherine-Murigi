ABOUT

What I actually do, and the tools I use to do it.

I'm an operations analyst who likes problems with a number attached. Give me a process that's slow, a report that takes too long to build, or a vendor relationship nobody's tracking — and I'll map it, measure it, and rebuild it so it holds up under real volume.

My background blends process improvement (Lean Six Sigma, SOP design, root-cause analysis) with hands-on data work (SQL, Python, Power BI). I'm usually the person translating between the floor and the spreadsheet — close enough to operations to know what's realistic, comfortable enough with data to prove it.

Outside of core analyst work, I write about process thinking, tooling tradeoffs, and the small habits that make operations data trustworthy — that's what the blog is for.
